Using

When useing the Virtual Breadboard you first select the tool which you wish to use. Then you click on the breadboard where you would like it to be placed. Each tool has different rules on how it is placed on the breadboard (Figure 3.1).

Tool How to use it
Chips Click on the pin on the breadboard where the chips top left pin (usualy pin 14) is to be placed. Chips may only be placed to span the gap so you can only select the pins in the bottom row on the top half.
Seven Segment Display Click on the pin on the breadboard where the seven segment displays pin 14 is to be placed. Seven segment displays may only be placed to span the gap so you can only select the pins in the bottom row on the top half
LED Click on the pin on the breadboard that you want the positive end of the LED to be connected to then click the pin on the breadboard that you want the negetive end to be connected to. Since LEDs are diode this does matter. Electricity will flow only form positive to negitive.
Button Click on the pin on the breadboard where you want one end of the button to be then click where you want the other to be.
Resistor Click on the pin on the breadboard where you want one end of the button to be then click where you want the other to be.
Wire Click on the pin on the breadboard where you want one end of the button to be then click where you want the other to be.
Tester Click on the pin on the breadboard to see the voltage and other useful infomation.
Figure 3.1: The breadboard is the component where all your circuitry is tested

Using the proporties of each tool supplied in "The components" section wire up your breadboard.

Once every thing is hooked up the way you want it just hit the power dipswitch (beside the power LED and lable). This will execute your breadboard.
